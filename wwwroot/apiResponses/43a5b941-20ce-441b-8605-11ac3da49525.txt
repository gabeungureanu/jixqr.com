Maria sat nervously in the small coffee shop, anxiously waiting for her best friend Susan to arrive. They hadn't seen each other in months, and Maria had a lot to catch her up on. As she stirred her cappuccino, she gazed out the window, watching the rain pour down and the passersby dart for cover.

Suddenly, the door burst open and in walked Susan, her long blonde hair damp from the rain. "Maria!" she exclaimed, rushing over to give her friend a hug. "It's been too long."

Maria smiled, feeling the warmth of her friend's embrace. "I know, I've missed you," she said.

They ordered their drinks and settled in at a small table, catching up on each other's lives. Susan listened intently as Maria talked about work, her new boyfriend, and her latest hobby – coding.

"Coding?" Susan repeated, looking surprised.

Maria nodded. "Yeah, I've been teaching myself C#. It's been challenging, but I love it."

Susan raised an eyebrow. "I don't know anything about coding. What's so great about it?"

Maria grinned, excited to share her passion. "It's like solving puzzles. You start with a problem and use code to figure out the solution. It's amazing to see what you can create just by typing on your computer."

Susan tilted her head, trying to understand. "But doesn't it get boring?"

Maria shook her head, grinning. "Never. There's always something new to learn or a new challenge to tackle. And the best part is, the possibilities are endless."

Susan leaned back in her chair, looking thoughtful. "I never thought of coding that way. Maybe I should try it."

Maria beamed. "Yes! You should. It's not as hard as it seems, and it can be so rewarding."

As they chatted and sipped their drinks, Maria realized that her love for coding went beyond the technical aspects. It was the feeling of accomplishment and the satisfaction of solving a complex problem that filled her with joy. And now, she had sparked the interest of her best friend, introducing her to a world of endless possibilities.

Through engaging dialogue, vivid descriptions, and a focus on character development, this scene illustrates the excitement and passion that coding can bring. By showing rather than telling, readers can feel the enthusiasm that Maria has for her craft and understand why it's so important to her. This 'show, don't tell' style of writing immerses the reader in the conversation, making it more engaging and memorable.
